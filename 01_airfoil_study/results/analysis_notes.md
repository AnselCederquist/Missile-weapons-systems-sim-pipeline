Top Left — CL vs CD (Drag Polar)

What it shows: Lift generated for a given amount of drag. Further left = less drag at same lift = more efficient.



Red (0006) sits furthest left — lowest drag, thinnest airfoil

Blue (0015) sits furthest right — highest drag, thickest airfoil

Green (0012) and yellow (0008) are in between

All four reach similar CLmax around 1.0





Top Middle — CL vs Alpha

What it shows: How lift increases with angle of attack. The slope is nearly identical for all four — that's thin airfoil theory, thickness doesn't change lift slope much. But stall angle differs.



Thicker airfoils (0015, 0012) stall at higher AoA — more gradual stall behavior

Thinner airfoils (0006, 0008) stall earlier and more abruptly

All four are linear up to about 8-10°





Top Right — Cm vs Alpha (Pitching Moment)

What it shows: Tendency of the airfoil to pitch nose-up or nose-down. For a symmetric airfoil at zero AoA, Cm should be near zero — and it is for all four, confirming they're symmetric. The noise at high AoA is post-stall instability.



Bottom Middle — CL vs Xtr (Transition Location)

What it shows: Where laminar flow transitions to turbulent flow on the upper surface. Further right = laminar flow maintained longer = less drag.



At low AoA, transition stays near the trailing edge (flow stays laminar longer)

As AoA increases, transition moves forward rapidly — flow trips turbulent sooner

Thinner airfoils transition further back at low AoA





What This Tells Us for Missile Canard Selection



Airfoil	Drag	CLmax	Stall behavior	Structural depth

&#x09;

0006	Lowest	\~0.85	Abrupt		Shallowest — weakest

0008	Low	\~0.95	Moderate	Moderate

0012	Medium	\~1.05	Gradual		Good

0015	Highest	\~1.05	Most gradual	Deepest — strongest



For a missile canard the tradeoff is: you want low drag (favor thin) but you also need structural depth for the fin spar and need predictable stall behavior for control authority. NACA 0008 or 0012 is typically the sweet spot — low enough drag, enough structural depth, gradual stall.
